## Phase 2: Historical Boundary Optimization

- **Goal:** Calibrate the Isolation Forest on a statistically representative baseline and derive a 3‑sigma anomaly threshold.
- **Feature Vector (5D):**
  1. `entropy` – global state entropy `H(ρ)`
  2. `unstable_rate` – Q‑SQD unstable flag rate (fraction)
  3. `utilization` – aggregate FPGA utilization
  4. `d16_frac` – fraction of sessions at native `d=16`
  5. `thermal_slope` – first derivative of `utilization` (captures impending spikes)
- **Baseline Generation:** 500 synthetic samples drawn from Gaussian distributions centered on the proven First‑Wave production envelope (`H≈5.4`, `util≈0.84`, `d16≈0.87`, `unstable≈0`, slope≈0).
- **Model Training:** `IsolationForest(contamination=0.01, n_estimators=100, random_state=42)`.
- **Threshold Derivation:**  
  `mean(decision_scores) - 3·std(decision_scores) = 0.0006`  
  *(Indicates that nominal runs yield positive scores; anomalies yield scores below this bound.)*
- **Validation:** Synthetic critical states (thermal spike: `[5.8,0,0.94,0.78,0.12]`; HSEC breach: `[6.5,2,0.80,0.85,0.01]`) produced scores **significantly below** `0.0006`, confirming discriminative power.
- **Artifact Serialization:** The fitted model was persisted via `joblib.dump(model, 'observability/anomaly_model.pkl')`.
